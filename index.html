Where do Suger gliders come from?

Sugar gliders come from 3 places in the world. Sugar gliders are native to the forests of New Guinea, Australia, and parts of Indonesia. These small, nocturnal marsupials are arboreal gliders. They're commonly found in the wild in the rainforests of these regions. I remember they’re part of the possum family and are known for their ability to glide. It's important to note that they're often kept as pets, even though their origins are in the wild.
